Audio samples from "Stress Detection and Transfer in Speech-to-Speech Machine Translation"

Abstract: The language diversity in India's education sector poses a significant challenge, hindering inclusivity. Despite the democratization of knowledge through online educational content, the dominance of English, as the internet's lingua franca, limits accessibility, emphasizing the crucial need for translation into Indian languages. Despite existing Speech to Speech Machine Translation (SSMT) technologies, the lack of intonation often leads to monotonous translations, leading to a loss of audience interest and disengagement from the content. To address this, our paper introduces a comprehensive dataset with stress annotations in Indian English for training a stress detection model and proposes a novel Text-to-Speech (TTS) architecture capable of incorporating stress into synthesized speech. The TTS architecture is based on FastPitch and can modify the variances based on stressed words given. We present an Indian English-to-Hindi SSMT system incorporating the proposed changes and aim to enhance the overall quality and engagement of educational content.
